## The journey

I started as a **mathematician**, spent years as a **science and mathematics teacher**, and moved into **data science and AI research** — earning an MS in Data Science as batch topper (CGPA 4.0/4.0) at the Riphah School of Computing & Innovation, Lahore. That path shapes how I work: mathematical grounding, a teacher's instinct for explaining hard ideas clearly, and a researcher's discipline about evidence and honesty.

The thread running through all of it is a preference for work that reaches people who are usually left out — which is why my research focuses on AI that runs on modest hardware, under real privacy and resource constraints, rather than only in well-funded labs.

## Teaching & experience

I have **8+ years of teaching experience**, currently as a Science & Mathematics teacher on a **BPS-14 substantive civil-service post** (awarded by competitive examination) — held concurrently with my MS studies, thesis, and four peer-reviewed papers. I have also taught at intermediate and undergraduate level and trained professionals in digital skills.

I received the **Best Teaching Performance Award** from the Progressive Education Network (PEN) NGO, Lahore (2024). Beyond the classroom, I served as **Vice President of the Tameer Kadah Foundation** (2018–2020), supporting welfare and development initiatives for children with physical and cognitive disabilities.

Teaching is not a side note to my research profile — it is where the communication, discipline, and mentoring that research depends on were built. Full history is on the [CV](/cv/) page.
